Design Grows From Nature

Bonsai trees are popular nowadays in social media. They are not genetically dwarfed plants— they are full-size trees kept small by planting them in small pots.

Welcome to Bonsai Gallery

Given the proper care, they can live for hundreds of years—passed on from generation to generation.

This project aims to help people who are unfamiliar with bonsai varieties, especially beginners who want to grow them at home.

Juniper Bonsai

Juniperus is a vast genus of evergreen coniferous trees and shrubs used as bonsai trees. All species can be grown successfully.

Chinese Juniper

This small bonsai endures climate changes, prefers full sun, and thrives in well-drained soil. It should be pruned carefully.

The Chinese elm bonsai is admired for its fine ramification, small leaves, and beautiful form.

The Jade Bonsai (Dwarf Jade) has succulent leaves and branches that turn reddish and woody with age.

Azalea Bonsai are beloved for their striking floral displays and are great for those who enjoy vibrant color.

Also known as Fukien Tea, the Carmona bonsai thrives indoors and produces small white flowers.
